Kids Don't Choose their Insurance.

In the U.S., most people are reliant on insurance to get medically necessary benefits. This doesn't always work. In the U.S., 10.2% of people are uninsured. Even for those who are insured, 30% of claims are “denied, lost, or ignored”. Kids have no ability to defend themselves against denials, or to pick an insurance policy. That's why at Orthotful, we’ve decided to provide a solution to help ensure kids get the care they need--if their care team says they need an orthotic device, insurance shouldn't stand in the way. We partner with Orthotists to provide an alternative payment solution for medically necessary orthotic devices for children.

How Orthotful Works:

We take patient privacy and the trust of our donors seriously, so we do not provide funds to patients directly at any time. Instead, we partner with the offices of qualified Orthotists (licensing requirements vary by state). They notify us when they have a patient that they think will be a good fit for our program, complete an application which confirms medical necessity and that they’ve attempted to follow all reasonable avenues to funding from other sources. We review the application, if approved, we send the Orthotist's office the funds needed to help the recipient. Because we are a nonprofit and our goal is to use our funds to do the most good possible, our payment rates are lower than most insurance companies. Therefore, orthotists are still incentivized to use the patient’s insurance as effectively as possible.

Our Criteria

We provide coverage for items that are Medically Necessary according to the patient's doctor and care team, only if the family has demonstrable financial need and either the patient's insurance has denied the device as a non-covered service, or the patient is uninsured.
